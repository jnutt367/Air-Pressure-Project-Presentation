Eighth Grade Science Project

Hypothesis

If the air pressure inside a football increases, then the distance the football travels when thrown will increase, because a properly inflated football keeps its shape and transfers more energy from the thrower’s arm into forward motion.

Conclusion

The results showed that the footballs with higher air pressure did not travel farther when thrown. Instead, footballs with lower or moderate air pressure often traveled a greater distance. This means the hypothesis was not supported. One possible reason is that an overinflated football may be harder to grip or control, which could reduce throwing accuracy and speed.
